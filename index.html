In Nigeria, the presidential system of government is being practised. In this system, even though democracy is in operation, there are three arms of government namely: the Legislature, the Executive, and the Judiciary. In the presidential system of government, the President has executive powers but there are some checks in the system so that he does not become a dictator.

The Legislature is concerned with making laws which the Executive exucutes(carries out). In other words, the Executive cannot make laws of its own and it must not go beyond the laws made by the Legislature. The Executive is also responsible for the day-to-day running of the affairs of government. The Legislature cannot interfere in this. The third term arm of government is the judiciary. The Judiciary interprets the laws made by the legislature and it sees to it that the Executive does not violate the laws. In other words, we can regard the Judiciary as the umpire. It sees that no arm of government violates the law or the constitution.

The government of the Federal Republic of Nigeria consists of three tiers; Federal, State and Local. The Federal Government is headed by the President, the State Government is headed by the Governor while the Chairman is the head of the Local Government. Each of the Tier has its roles spelt out in the constitution
